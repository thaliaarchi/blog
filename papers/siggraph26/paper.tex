\section{UNIX History}

UNIX is an influential time-sharing operating system created by Ken Thompson,
Dennis Ritchie, and others at Bell Laboratories in the 1970s. After the shutdown
of the ambitious Multics project, Thompson started work on an operating system
for a disused PDP-7 computer. The limitations of this small machine drove the
innovations of UNIX, by necessity. UNIX distilled the design of Multics to its
necessary core. Soon its potential was recognized by Doug McIlroy and they
secured funding to develop a word processing system for the Patent department.
With these funds, they acquired a new PDP-11 machine.
